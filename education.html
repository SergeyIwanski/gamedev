Decision Support System

Yes, we had such a subject in our 5th year. The professor - head of department puzzled us to modify the system so that you can enter an infinite number of actions and events for all types of tasks. Failure to fulfill the professor’s condition meant completely failing the subject and, instead of graduating from university, staying for another year for retake. I often saw students who could not complete their studies, because they were forced to come to him to resubmit their work, with new additional conditions.

 

In the task "Wheel of Fortune" for actions, okay, I can set the numerical value of speed from 1rpm to infinity, but how to generate an infinite number of colors ?? How many color names can you remember? Red, blue, green, purple, gold, transparent, gray-brown-raspberry...

What will be 1 000 000+ color? More importantly, how to program this to show a completed work before the end of the week?

 

And I found a solution!

I introduced a fractions of the color. That is, the three components are red, green and blue in a ratio of 1: 1: 1, then I alternately added +1 red (2:1:1,  3:1:1,  etc.) Thus it turns out an infinite number of shades of red. I do not know how correct this is, but the professor agreed with this decision.

 

Further there was such a problem. It was necessary to calculate the combination of the bone rolls, but after the 8th roll MS Access reached the internal software limit, the file quickly gained 2GB and became inoperative, forever.

 

Before us, students solved this problem using Visual Basic Application, increasing the number of throws to 10. Since these works were freely available and everyone simply copied ready-made solutions, the professor said that he would underestimate the grades of those who use Visual Basic. Therefore, I decided to do everything through queries, only with Access tools. The professor also set the condition to break their record, otherwise an average grade is guaranteed.

 

Then I ran into another problem, this is the Poisson formula for probability distribution.

The main snag here in the factorial. The fact is that in Access expressions there is no factorial function, only using Access cannot create a recursive function, there is not even the opportunity to create a variable for the increment i. I even found on the net a replacement for the Poisson formula without factorial. But the error was too great.

 

I have already done a lot of work, too much time has passed. I scolded myself for not using the ready-made solution, as many did. How many problems I could avoid!

 

Once an idea came to me. Yes, I can’t just change the data, because the old records will remain and the new ones will be added to the end. Then I began to delete everything!  I specifically made a separate tables. For the factorial, I developed a certain sequence of actions and recorded them in a macro. Before each factorial calculation, it is necessary to initialize the table responsible for this, i.e. to clear and set a initial values.

 

Also, through intermediate tables, I solved the problem with the number of throws, up to 22!

Theoretically, much more is possible. It just took a computer many hours to calculate the solution to these 22 throws, and adding a new throw increases the calculation time by an order of magnitude.

 

 

 

Graduate work

When I asked if I could make a game for my graduation project, the department head allowed but said that it should solve an important problem or business task:)

Then I decided to make an intelligence training game. The game included sections such as deduction, speed of reaction, intuition... That's what I got to train a memory:

 

The professor required scientific credibility to estimate IQ. After consulting with the psychologist working there, and searching the Internet, it was not so simple, to study this issue I would need to spend a few years studying at the Institute of Psychology (and it is possible to write a separate scientific work for this)! In short, my work failed.

 

Having gathered with the teachers, having consulted, we found a solution not to make a game, but something that would help with its development. The decision is actually dubious because it is not a ready-made game or software. It was supposed to be an asset from a universal set of elements from which, like lego, you can quickly make a finished game. And everything that I did before was used.

 

Alas, the volume of work turned out to be too large. Perhaps the long days of lack of sleep affected, I did not have time to do much of what I planned. Then Indira Rafikovna told me not to worry and continue to do what I could. She told me that we are not gods, that we cannot know everything and are not obliged to do everything.

 

I continued to work. I decided to make every effort to the very end, just to know that I was defeated not because I gave up or because I was too lazy.

 

The day before graduation, I was running around the city to collect signatures and pass a review. I was upset that I couldn’t even prepare for the presentation because of this damned red tape! In the morning I was told to make an annotation in another language. Damn it! Could not say before?! There was no time to think, I just copied someone else's text and guessed which words needed to be replaced, and it turned out to be right!

 

My graduation project had 116 pages printed on a color printer. At the end of the day I gave my work to the master for binding. There were other works in red book covers lying in rows. It takes a lot of time. I could get my work only tomorrow.

Graduation Day

We were told to arrive early to go through the last reviewer. Okay, a few hours should be enough. I prepared everything for demonstration and fixed the last bugs (at 4 a.m.).

 

I managed to get some sleep, and arrived at the university on time. Took my "Red Book" :)

The censor arrived only after a some hours of the appointed time, in addition, she yelled at us, that we had to submit the work for review a week ago. The teachers also told us to make a printout of the presentation for each member of the commission, saying that it is so convenient to be able to view it close on paper. Instead of letting us get ready to present our work. Having cursed well, I went to the nearest store.

 

My presentation contained 69 slides. The printer operator (seller) ran out of paper, there were no paper in the nearest stores either. But I quickly found one inside the university and bought a whole stack of paper. I borrowed money from students for printing, I dialed the required amount.

 

The printout takes a very long time, the graduation has long been in full swing. I was asked where my printouts are, why so long. When I said how many pages there, they allowed me to reduce to 2 copies. I suggested the seller that he would print in exchange for the remaining sheets of paper. Then I returned all my debts to the students. Such a business I made :)

 

Together with us, students who were supposed to finish their studies last year presented their works. The commission members were already bored and perked up when I began to talk about my work, about what I did. Especially during the demonstration. I was not worried, I was pleased to present the results of my work!

 

After the meeting, we were called to announce ratings. Only 3 students received the highest score (100). And I was one of them!

 

Interestingly, no one needed the finished game. But in a disassembled form, not yet completely ready, representing only a set of its constituent elements, it turned out to be quite appreciated. Paradox!

 

Some time after everything, I tried to continue my work on the project and then I realized the impossibility of my idea. Instead of one complex system, it would be better to make many small simple assets, which I did later...
